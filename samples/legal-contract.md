## 2. TERM AND TERMINATION

### 2.1 Initial Term

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for an initial period of twenty-four (24) months (the **"Initial Term"**), unless terminated earlier in accordance with this Clause 2.

### 2.2 Renewal

Upon expiry of the Initial Term, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive periods of twelve (12) months (each a **"Renewal Term"**) unless either Party gives the other not less than ninety (90) days' written notice of its intention not to renew.

### 2.3 Termination for Convenience

Either Party may terminate this Agreement at any time by giving one hundred and eighty (180) days' written notice to the other Party. During the notice period, the Company shall continue to provide the Services, and the Client shall continue to pay the Fees, in accordance with the terms of this Agreement.

### 2.4 Termination for Cause

Either Party may terminate this Agreement with immediate effect by written notice to the other Party if:

(a) **Material Breach:** The other Party commits a material breach of this Agreement and, if the breach is capable of remedy, fails to remedy it within thirty (30) days of receiving written notice specifying the breach and requiring its remedy;

(b) **Insolvency:** The other Party becomes insolvent, makes an arrangement with its creditors, has a receiver, administrator, or liquidator appointed, or ceases to carry on business; or

(c) **Illegality:** It becomes unlawful for either Party to perform its obligations under this Agreement.

### 2.5 Consequences of Termination

Upon termination or expiry of this Agreement:

(a) The Company shall deliver to the Client all completed Deliverables for which payment has been received and all work in progress on uncompleted Deliverables;

(b) The Client shall pay the Company all outstanding Fees for Services rendered up to the date of termination, including any non-cancellable costs incurred by the Company;

(c) Each Party shall return or destroy (at the other Party's election) all Confidential Information belonging to the other Party;

(d) Clauses 1 (Definitions), 6 (Confidentiality), 7 (Intellectual Property), 8 (Limitation of Liability), and 11 (Governing Law) shall survive termination.

## 3. SERVICES AND DELIVERABLES

### 3.1 Statement of Work

The Company shall provide the Services and deliver the Deliverables described in each Statement of Work, which shall be substantially in the form set out in Schedule 1. Each SOW shall specify:

(a) A detailed description of the Services;
(b) The Deliverables to be produced;
(c) The timeline for completion, including milestones;
(d) The Fees payable and the payment schedule;
(e) The key personnel assigned to the engagement;
(f) Any assumptions, dependencies, or constraints; and
(g) Any Client resources or information required.

### 3.2 Standard of Care

The Company shall perform the Services with reasonable skill, care, and diligence, in accordance with:

(a) The standards generally observed in the information technology and professional services industry;
(b) Applicable Law; and
(c) The terms of this Agreement and the relevant SOW.

### 3.3 Change Orders

Either Party may propose changes to the scope, timeline, or fees of any SOW by submitting a written Change Order request. No change shall be effective until both Parties have executed a Change Order in writing. The Company shall not be obliged to commence work on a Change Order until the Parties have agreed on any adjustment to the Fees and timeline.

### 3.4 Client Obligations

The Client shall:

(a) Provide the Company with timely access to its personnel, facilities, systems, and information as reasonably required for the performance of the Services;
(b) Appoint a project manager who shall be the primary point of contact for the Company;
(c) Review and provide feedback on Deliverables within the timeframes specified in the relevant SOW; and
(d) Ensure that any materials or data provided to the Company do not infringe the Intellectual Property Rights of any third party.

### 3.5 Acceptance

Deliverables shall be deemed accepted if the Client does not provide written notice of rejection specifying the reasons for rejection within fifteen (15) Business Days of delivery. If the Client rejects a Deliverable, the Company shall have thirty (30) Business Days to remedy the identified deficiencies and resubmit the Deliverable at no additional cost.

## 4. FEES, EXPENSES, AND PAYMENT

### 4.1 Fees

The Client shall pay the Company the fees set out in each Statement of Work. Unless otherwise specified in the SOW, fees are exclusive of applicable taxes, duties, and levies, which shall be borne by the Client. Fees are non-refundable.

### 4.2 Expenses

The Client shall reimburse the Company for all reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred in the performance of the Services, including travel, accommodation, and subsistence, provided that the Company submits receipts or other reasonable evidence of such expenses.

### 4.3 Invoicing and Payment

The Company shall invoice the Client in accordance with the payment schedule specified in the relevant SOW. The Client shall pay each invoice within thirty (30) days of receipt. Late payments shall bear interest at the rate of 2% per month, or the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, whichever is lower, calculated from the due date until the date of actual payment.

### 4.4 Disputed Invoices

If the Client disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, it shall pay the undisputed portion by the due date and notify the Company in writing of the nature and amount of the dispute within ten (10) Business Days of receipt of the invoice. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve the dispute within thirty (30) days.

### 4.5 Suspension of Services

If the Client fails to pay any undisputed amount by its due date, the Company may, upon ten (10) Business Days' written notice, suspend the provision of Services until all outstanding amounts are paid in full. The Company shall not be liable for any loss or damage arising from such suspension.

## 6. CONFIDENTIALITY

### 6.1 Confidentiality Obligations

Each Party undertakes to the other that it shall:

(a) Keep all Confidential Information strictly confidential;
(b) Use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing its obligations or exercising its rights under this Agreement;
(c) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the disclosing Party's prior written consent; and
(d) Limit access to Confidential Information to its employees, officers, and subcontractors who have a genuine need to know and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less stringent than those set out in this Clause 6.

### 6.2 Exceptions

The obligations in Clause 6.1 shall not apply to Confidential Information that:

(a) Is or becomes publicly available other than through a breach of this Agreement;
(b) Was already lawfully in the recipient's possession without an obligation of confidentiality prior to its disclosure;
(c) Is independently developed by the recipient without use of or reference to the disclosing Party's Confidential Information; or
(d) Is required to be disclosed by Applicable Law or by order of a court or regulatory authority of competent jurisdiction, provided that the recipient gives the disclosing Party reasonable prior notice and cooperates with any efforts to limit or prevent the disclosure.

## 7. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### 7.1 Background IP

Each Party retains ownership of its Background IP. No licence to Background IP is granted except to the extent necessary for the performance of the Services.

### 7.2 Foreground IP

Unless otherwise agreed in writing, all Foreground IP shall vest in the Client upon full payment of all Fees due in respect of the Services that generated the Foreground IP. The Company hereby assigns (and shall procure that its personnel assign) to the Client all right, title, and interest in and to the Foreground IP.

### 7.3 Licence to Company

The Client grants the Company a non-exclusive, royalty-free, worldwide licence to use the Foreground IP for the sole purpose of providing the Services. The Company retains a perpetual, non-exclusive, royalty-free licence to use general ideas, concepts, know-how, methodologies, and techniques developed in the course of providing the Services, provided that such use does not disclose the Client's Confidential Information.

## 8. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

### 8.1 Exclusion of Certain Damages

**NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR ANY INDIRECT, CONSEQUENTIAL, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOSS OF PROFITS, LOSS OF BUSINESS, LOSS OF DATA, OR LOSS OF GOODWILL, HOWSOEVER ARISING, WHETHER IN CONTRACT, TORT (INCLUDING NEGLIGENCE), OR OTHERWISE, EVEN IF THE PARTY HAS BEEN ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.**

### 8.2 Cap on Liability

**SUBJECT TO CLAUSE 8.3, THE TOTAL AGGREGATE LIABILITY OF EITHER PARTY TO THE OTHER UNDER OR IN CONNECTION WITH THIS AGREEMENT, WHETHER IN CONTRACT, TORT, OR OTHERWISE, SHALL NOT EXCEED THE TOTAL FEES PAID OR PAYABLE BY THE CLIENT TO THE COMPANY DURING THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING THE EVENT GIVING RISE TO THE LIABILITY.**

### 8.3 Unlimited Liability

Nothing in this Agreement shall limit either Party's liability for:

(a) Death or personal injury caused by negligence;
(b) Fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation;
(c) Breach of the confidentiality obligations in Clause 6;
(d) Infringement of the other Party's Intellectual Property Rights; or
(e) Any liability that cannot be excluded or limited by Applicable Law.

## 9. INDEMNIFICATION

### 9.1 IP Infringement Indemnity

The Company shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Client against any claim, suit, or proceeding brought against the Client alleging that the Deliverables infringe a third party's Intellectual Property Rights, provided that the Client:

(a) Notifies the Company promptly in writing of the claim;
(b) Gives the Company sole control of the defence and settlement negotiations; and
(c) Provides reasonable cooperation and assistance, at the Company's expense.

### 9.2 Mitigation

If any Deliverable is held or is reasonably likely to be held to infringe a third party's Intellectual Property Rights, the Company may, at its option and expense:

(a) Procure the right for the Client to continue using the Deliverable;
(b) Modify the Deliverable so that it becomes non-infringing while maintaining substantially equivalent functionality; or
(c) If (a) and (b) are not commercially reasonable, terminate the relevant SOW and refund the Fees paid for the infringing Deliverable.

## 10. INSURANCE

### 10.1 Insurance Requirements

The Company shall maintain in force throughout the Term:

(a) Professional indemnity insurance with a limit of not less than ₦50,000,000 (fifty million Naira) per claim and in the aggregate;
(b) Public liability insurance with a limit of not less than ₦20,000,000 (twenty million Naira) per occurrence; and
(c) Employers' liability insurance as required by Applicable Law.

### 10.2 Evidence

The Company shall provide the Client with evidence of such insurance upon request.

## 11. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### 11.1 Governing Law

This Agreement and any dispute or claim arising out of or in connection with it shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Federal Republic of Nigeria.

### 11.2 Amicable Resolution

The Parties shall attempt in good faith to resolve any dispute arising under this Agreement through negotiations between senior representatives of each Party within thirty (30) days of a Party giving notice of the dispute.

### 11.3 Mediation

If the dispute is not resolved by negotiation within thirty (30) days, the Parties shall refer the dispute to mediation administered by the Lagos Multi-Door Courthouse (LMDC) in accordance with its Mediation Rules. Unless otherwise agreed, the mediation shall take place in Lagos, Nigeria.

### 11.4 Arbitration

If the dispute is not resolved by mediation within forty-five (45) days of the appointment of a mediator, either Party may refer the dispute to arbitration in accordance with the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, Cap A18, Laws of the Federation of Nigeria, 2004. The arbitration shall be conducted by a single arbitrator mutually agreed by the Parties or, failing agreement, appointed by the President of the Chartered Institute of Arbitrators (Nigeria Branch). The seat of arbitration shall be Lagos, Nigeria, and the language of the arbitration shall be English.

### 11.5 Court Proceedings

Notwithstanding Clauses 11.2 to 11.4, either Party may at any time seek injunctive or other equitable relief from a court of competent jurisdiction to protect its Confidential Information or Intellectual Property Rights.

## 12. GENERAL PROVISIONS

### 12.1 Entire Agreement

This Agreement, together with all Statements of Work and Change Orders executed pursuant to it, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to its subject matter and supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, and representations, whether oral or written.

### 12.2 Variation

No variation of this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing and signed by duly authorised representatives of both Parties.

### 12.3 Waiver

No failure or delay by either Party in exercising any right under this Agreement shall operate as a waiver of that right. Any waiver must be in writing and signed by the waiving Party.

### 12.4 Severance

If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect. The Parties shall negotiate in good faith to replace the invalid provision with a valid provision that most closely reflects the original intent.

### 12.5 Assignment

Neither Party may assign its rights or obligations under this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other Party, except that either Party may assign to an Affiliate or in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all of its assets.

### 12.6 Subcontracting

The Company may subcontract portions of the Services, provided that the Company remains primarily liable for the performance of the subcontractor and shall ensure that the subcontractor is bound by confidentiality obligations consistent with this Agreement.

### 12.7 Notices

All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered personally, by registered post, or by email to the addresses set out at the head of this Agreement, or to such other address as a Party may notify from time to time. Notices shall be deemed received:

(a) If delivered personally, at the time of delivery;
(b) If sent by registered post, five (5) Business Days after posting; and
(c) If sent by email, at the time of transmission, provided that no error message is received.

### 12.8 Force Majeure

Neither Party shall be liable for any delay or failure in performance caused by a Force Majeure Event. The affected Party shall notify the other Party promptly of the Force Majeure Event and its expected duration. If the Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) days, either Party may terminate the affected SOW without liability.

### 12.9 Relationship of the Parties

The Parties are independent contractors. Nothing in this Agreement shall create a partnership, joint venture, agency, or employment relationship between the Parties.

### 12.10 Third Party Rights

A person who is not a Party to this Agreement shall have no right under the Contracts (Rights of Third Parties) Act to enforce any term of this Agreement.

### 12.11 Counterparts

This Agreement may be executed in any number of counterparts, each of which shall be an original, and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument.
